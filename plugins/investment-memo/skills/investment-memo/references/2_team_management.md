## 2. Team & Management

> **Assessment Pillar:** Management | **Weight:** Seed 25% / Series A 20%
>
> *"We invest in people."* — At early stage, the team IS the asset. This chapter assesses whether the founding team can execute against the identified opportunity under uncertainty, pivot when needed, and attract the talent required to scale.

### 2.1 — Founder-Market Fit [0–5] 🚩 RED FLAG

The founders possess deep domain expertise, relevant industry networks, and lived experience that uniquely position them to solve this specific problem. They hold an authentic, earned "unfair insight" — first-hand knowledge that outsiders would struggle to replicate within 6 months — providing a durable advantage in understanding the customer pain, market dynamics, and solution requirements.

**How to assess (junior analyst checklist):**
- Review each founder's LinkedIn, publications, patents, and prior roles — map years of domain experience to the specific problem space.
- Ask: "Why are YOU the right team for this?" and probe for first-hand stories, not generic answers.
- Check whether the founding insight came from direct exposure (e.g., worked at the customer, saw the pain) or from desk research (weaker signal).
- Identify the "unfair insight" — what does this team know about the problem that a smart generalist could not learn in 6 months?
- Cross-reference with reference checks (2.6): Do references confirm the claimed domain expertise?

> *Stage note: At Seed, founder-market fit often compensates for limited traction. At Series A, it must be validated by execution results.*

---

### 2.2 — Team Complementarity [0–5] 🚩 RED FLAG

The founding team covers all three critical early-stage functions — Technology/Product, Commercial/GTM, and Operations/Finance — through complementary founders without critical single-point-of-failure gaps. Co-founders have a stress-tested working relationship, align on vision, and complement each other on execution approach.

**How to assess (junior analyst checklist):**
- Map each founder to a primary function: Tech/Product, Commercial/GTM, Ops/Finance. Identify any function without clear ownership.
- For each gap: Is there a committed hire (signed offer letter or named candidate) or is it aspirational?
- Evaluate co-founder relationship history: How long have they known each other? Have they worked together before under pressure? Co-founder blowups are a top startup death cause — probe for evidence of stress-tested collaboration.
- Check working styles: Do founders agree on vision but complement on execution approach? Or are there unresolved strategic disagreements?

> *Red Flag triggers: Solo founder without complementary hires committed. Two technical co-founders with zero commercial capability and no GTM hire planned.*

---

### 2.3 — Track Record & Commitment [0–5]

The founders demonstrate prior relevant execution credibility through verifiable achievements — including prior exits, relevant industry roles, technical accomplishments (patents, publications), or a demonstrated ability to ship product. Second-time founders articulate concrete lessons from prior successes or failures that directly inform their current approach. All founders are fully dedicated to the venture, accepting below-market salaries as a commitment signal, and have invested personal capital where applicable. The opportunity cost each founder bears — relative to their market alternatives — demonstrates genuine conviction in the venture's potential.

**How to assess (junior analyst checklist):**

**Track Record Assessment:**
- For each founder: Build a timeline of 3–5 most relevant professional achievements with verifiable outcomes (revenue grown, product shipped, patent granted, team built).
- Search Crunchbase / LinkedIn for prior startup involvement — check outcomes (exit, shutdown, still running).
- For academic founders: Count publications in top-tier venues, h-index, citation counts, invited talks. Are they recognized leaders in their subfield?
- For industry founders: What was their scope of responsibility (budget, team size, P&L)? Did they build something from zero or manage an existing operation?
- Ask: "Tell me about a project that failed and what you learned." Ability to articulate failure lessons is a positive signal.

**Commitment & Skin in the Game Assessment:**
- Confirm full-time status for each founder directly — ask: "Are any founders still employed elsewhere, consulting, or running another project?" Document the answer.
- Ask for current founder salaries. Compare to market rate for their seniority. Significant below-market salary (e.g., €60K vs. €120K market) is a positive commitment signal.
- Determine if founders have invested personal capital — even small amounts signal conviction.
- Assess opportunity cost: A founder who left a €200K+ senior role at Google carries higher conviction signal than one without alternatives.
- Check that employment contracts with the startup are in place (or planned pre-close).

> *Red Flag indicators: Founders not full-time. Founders maintaining parallel consulting or employment.*
>
> *Scoring guidance: Score reflects the weaker of (a) track record credibility and (b) commitment signals. A founder with stellar track record but part-time involvement scores low. A fully committed founder with weak track record scores moderately.*

### 2.4 — Resilience & Coachability [0–5]

The founders are receptive to feedback, willing to iterate on strategy based on data, and proactively transparent during the DD process — including volunteering bad news unprompted. Their behavior during DD serves as a reliable predictor of post-investment collaboration: they engage constructively with pushback on assumptions, share data room access promptly and completely, and demonstrate intellectual honesty about gaps and uncertainties. The founding team possesses the psychological capacity to endure a 7–10 year deep-tech journey — including fundraising rejection, technical failures, team conflict, and extended pre-revenue periods. Founders demonstrate clear self-awareness, articulate concrete stress-management strategies, show a track record of perseverance under adversity, and are open to executive coaching and structured support.

**How to assess (junior analyst checklist):**

**Coachability & Transparency Assessment:**
- During DD calls, deliberately challenge one or two key assumptions (e.g., market size, conversion rates, timeline). Document the response: Did the founder engage constructively, get defensive, or dismiss the concern?
- Ask for bad news proactively: "What's the biggest thing that's gone wrong in the last 6 months?" Founders who volunteer failures unprompted score higher.
- Check: Did the team share data room access promptly and completely — or was information drip-fed and incomplete?
- Reference-check specifically for coachability: "How does [Founder] respond when told they're wrong?"
- Post-investment predictor: The DD process itself is the best sample of post-investment founder behavior. Treat it as a live assessment.

**Founder Resilience & Psychological Readiness Assessment:**
- Probe for "outlier achievement" patterns: Has the founder previously persisted through extended adversity (e.g., completed a PhD under difficult conditions, built something over years with repeated setbacks, recovered from a business failure)?
- Ask directly: "What was the hardest professional period you've gone through, and how did you handle it?" Look for self-awareness and concrete coping strategies — not bravado.
- Assess self-awareness: Can the founder articulate their own weaknesses and blind spots without prompting? Do they acknowledge what they don't know?
- Evaluate leadership under stress: During the DD process, did any high-pressure moment arise (tight deadline, difficult question, co-founder disagreement)? How was it handled?
- Check support structures: Does the founder have a mentor, coach, peer group, or advisory board? Are they open to executive coaching post-investment?
- Reference-check specifically for resilience: "How does [Founder] handle extended periods of uncertainty or bad news?"

> *As the saying goes: "Wer in der DD nicht transparent ist, wird es nach dem Investment erst recht nicht sein."*
>
> *Research context: 72% of founders report negative mental health impacts from launching a business, making psychological readiness a critical assessment for deep-tech ventures with multi-year development cycles.*
>
> *Scoring guidance: Score reflects the weaker of (a) coachability & transparency and (b) psychological resilience. Score 5 if the founder demonstrates clear self-awareness, engages constructively with pushback, volunteers bad news unprompted, articulates concrete stress-management strategies, has a track record of perseverance under adversity, and is open to coaching support. Score 2–3 if resilience or coachability evidence is mixed or the founder lacks self-awareness but shows raw determination. Score 0–1 if the founder displays brittle reactions to pressure during DD, withholds information, has no support system, and refuses coaching.*
>
> *Best practice (Cherry Ventures / Eka Ventures): Leading European VCs now require newly-backed founders to engage with executive coaches for 12+ months and conduct structured 90-minute behavioral assessments including resilience and self-awareness dimensions.*

### 2.5 — Equity & Structure [0–5]

The founder equity split reflects contributions and alignment fairly, reverse vesting is in place (standard: 4-year, 1-year cliff), and the cap table is clean — no dead equity, no excessive early dilution from angel rounds or advisors, and no toxic preference stacks from prior rounds. The VSOP/ESOP structure is tax-optimized for the German context (Zukunftsfinanzierungsgesetz considered), and sufficient equity headroom (10–15%) is reserved for the next 3–5 critical hires. No critical knowledge, customer relationships, or technical capability is locked in a single individual without documentation or succession awareness. The team has a concrete hiring pipeline for the next 3–5 key positions, a demonstrated ability to attract A-players (network strength, employer brand, location attractiveness, equity pool), and awareness of German-specific labor law risks (works council formation under Betriebsverfassungsgesetz, employment protection under Kündigungsschutzgesetz).

**How to assess (junior analyst checklist):**

**Equity Structure, Vesting & German Corporate Specifics:**
- Request the current cap table. Identify: founder ownership (combined and individual), investor ownership, ESOP/VSOP pool size, any advisor or angel shares.
- Check: Is reverse vesting in place for ALL founders? Standard = 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff. If not, flag as a condition for investment.
- Calculate founder dilution path: After this round + a typical Series A (20–25% dilution) + Series B (15–20%), will founders still hold >25% combined? If not, incentive alignment is at risk.
- Identify any dead equity (shares held by departed co-founders or early contributors who are no longer active).
- **Germany-specific (critical):** Germany ranks 23rd out of 24 countries for stock option friendliness. Determine: Is the company using a Virtual Stock Option Plan (VSOP) or traditional ESOP? Over 80% of German startups use VSOPs because traditional options trigger "dry income" taxation — employees are taxed at fair market value on share receipt, before any liquidity event. Confirm: Has the company received legal advice on the optimal incentive structure? Are VSOP terms (valuation formula, exercise window, leaver provisions) market-standard? Has the company considered the 2024 Future Financing Act (Zukunftsfinanzierungsgesetz) changes to employee equity taxation?
- Check: Does the equity/VSOP pool have sufficient headroom (10–15% reserved) to hire the next 3–5 critical roles?

**Key Person Risk, Hiring Capacity & Labor Law Exposure:**
- Identify single-point-of-failure individuals: If [Person X] left tomorrow, what would break? Document for each key person.
- Assess hiring pipeline: Ask for the next 3–5 planned hires with role descriptions, timelines, and sourcing strategy. Has the team successfully recruited senior talent before?
- Evaluate employer brand: Is the company location attractive for talent (e.g., Munich, Berlin vs. rural)? Can they compete for deep-tech talent (materials scientists, ML engineers, hardware engineers) against corporates and Big Tech?
- Check VSOP/ESOP pool: Is there enough equity to attract senior hires?
- **Germany-specific — Works council risk (Betriebsrat):** A works council can be formed at any company with 5+ permanent employees — and management cannot prevent it. Once established, the works council must be consulted before ANY dismissal (§102 Works Constitution Act / Betriebsverfassungsgesetz). Currently only ~5% of Berlin startups have works councils, but formation can be triggered by a single employee. Assess: How many employees does the company have? Are any works council formation efforts underway? Has the company factored works council consultation into its HR processes?
- **German employment protection (Kündigungsschutzgesetz):** Germany has one of the strongest employment protection systems globally. Termination requires operational, personal, or behavioral reasons with formal prior warnings typically required. Special protection applies to pregnant employees, severely disabled employees, works council members, and employees on parental leave. Assess: Does the management team understand German labor law requirements? Do they have HR/legal counsel for employment matters?

> *Red Flag indicators: Cap table already diluted >40% pre-Seed. No vesting in place. Asymmetric equity split without clear rationale. ESOP used instead of VSOP without tax counsel advice (German companies). No equity pool reserved for future hires.*
>
> *Scoring guidance: Score reflects the weaker of (a) equity & governance quality (fair split, vesting, cap table cleanliness, tax optimization) and (b) key person & hiring risk (knowledge distribution, recruiting pipeline, labor law exposure). Score 5 if no single-point-of-failure, strong hiring pipeline with evidence of successful past recruiting, adequate equity pool, clean cap table with proper vesting, and labor law awareness. Score 2–3 if key person risk exists but is acknowledged with a mitigation plan, or if equity structure has minor issues. Score 0–1 if critical knowledge is locked in one person with no documentation, hiring plan, or succession awareness, or if cap table has severe structural problems.*

### 2.6 — Reference Checks [0–5]

Structured back-channel references (minimum 3 independent per key founder — from former colleagues, managers, co-founders, and critically investors who passed) consistently confirm the founders' self-narrative regarding competence, leadership style, and integrity. Pattern-matching across references reveals no material gaps between self-presentation and external perception.

**How to assess (junior analyst checklist):**
- Conduct minimum 3 independent references per key founder. Sources: former managers, peers, direct reports, co-founders from prior ventures, and at least 1 investor who passed on the deal.
- Use a consistent question set across references: (a) "What is [Founder]'s greatest strength?" (b) "What would you say is their biggest development area?" (c) "Would you work with / invest in them again? Why or why not?" (d) "How do they handle conflict and pressure?" (e) "How do they respond to being told they're wrong?"
- Document direct quotes verbatim — they are the most valuable output of reference checks.
- Pattern-match: Do references independently confirm or contradict the founder's self-narrative? Consistent gaps (e.g., multiple references flag delegation issues) are high-signal.
- Back-channel references (people the founder did NOT suggest) carry more weight than provided references.

> *Best practice (Social Capital / Groq memo style): Document direct quotes from references, e.g., "Best engineer I've ever managed" or "Brilliant but struggles to delegate."*

**Chapter 2 — Red Flags (automatic Pass triggers):**
- 🚩 Solo founder with no committed co-founder or complementary C-level hire
- 🚩 Founders not working full-time on the venture
- 🚩 Cap table diluted >40% before this round with no clean-up plan
- 🚩 Negative or inconsistent reference checks across multiple sources
- 🚩 Founder refuses transparency during DD or withholds material information
- 🚩 Founder displays brittle or erratic behavior under pressure during DD with no self-awareness or support structures
